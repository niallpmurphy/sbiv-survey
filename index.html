1. What sex-related inference, if any, did the paper make?

This includes claims of sex difference, no sex difference, similarity, sex-specific treatment effects, sex-specific mechanisms, or pooling/collapsing across sex.

Show Scientific Guidance & Examples

Where to look: Title, abstract, results, discussion, figure legends, tables, and supplementary material.

Sex difference claimed: Use when the paper explicitly argues that males and females differ.

No sex difference / similarity claimed: Use when the paper explicitly argues that males and females did not differ or were similar.

No sex-related inference: Use when sex is present only as a sample descriptor and no sex-related inference is made.

Unspecified / unclear: Use only when the available copy is too incomplete or unclear to judge.

2. Sex-Related Procedural Confounds

Show Scientific Guidance & Examples

Where to look: Methods sections such as housing, apparatus, procedure, design, scoring, or analysis.

What belongs here: Only procedural details that could create, mask, exaggerate, or mislead interpretation of an apparent sex difference.

Why this matters: Housing, odor, scheduling, sex-linked observer bias, and estrous handling can all shape outcomes and can be confused with sex effects if not described clearly.

3. Were sex-related confounders included in the analysis?

(e.g., body weight)

Show Scientific Guidance & Examples

Where to look: Usually in the statistical analysis, methods, covariates, model specification, or figure legend text.

Why this matters: Sex-related confounders such as body weight can influence outcomes and may need to be modeled or adjusted for to interpret sex effects responsibly.

In at least one case: Relevant confounders were included in at least one analysis, model, or outcome.

No cases: Relevant confounders were not included in any reported analysis.

Explicitly not included: The paper makes clear that relevant confounders were not included in the analysis.

Unspecified: The paper does not clearly say whether such confounders were included.

Not applicable: No relevant sex-related confounder appears applicable for that analysis.
